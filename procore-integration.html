SEC. 01  /  The round trip

Your GC stays in Procore.
You never live in it.

The GC’s question starts in their system and the answer lands back in their system — formal, attached, ball-in-court moved. Everything in between happens where your design team actually works.

They ask

An RFI or submittal opens in Procore — and shows up in BuildCycle with its attachments and approver workflow. Nothing forwarded, nothing retyped.

You route it

Send it to the right consultant by discipline. They answer inside BuildCycle — free, no license, no PDF ping-pong over email.

You approve

Review as the prime and stamp the answer. The paper trail — who answered what, when — builds itself while you work.

They get a formal answer

Your response posts back as an official Procore action and ball-in-court moves. To your GC, you’re just fast.

SEC. 02  /  How it works

Four steps.
No IT project.

Connect once per project. After that, the integration runs itself — sync in, respond, post back.

01

Connect

OAuth-link your Procore project from BuildCycle’s integrations page. It takes minutes — no IT project, no middleware, no consultants to hire.

OAuth 2.0 · Per-project link · Minutes to set up
02

Sync

Open RFIs and submittals — with their approver workflows and attachments — sync into BuildCycle on connect and refresh as you work the project.

On-connect sync · Approver workflows · Attachments
03

Respond

Route items to consultants by discipline, review as the prime, and approve — all inside BuildCycle, where your whole design team already works.

Discipline routing · Prime review · Approval
04

Post

Approved answers post back as formal Procore actions — RFI replies with attachments, and submittal approver responses (Approved / Approved as Noted / Revise & Resubmit / Rejected) that advance ball-in-court.

Formal actions · Ball-in-court advances
SEC. 03  /  Ground rules

Your GC’s record.
Still theirs.

The first question every GC asks: “is this going to touch our Procore?” Only in ways they’d want. Four rules the integration never breaks — forward this section to your GC.

Status stays yours

BuildCycle never modifies the status of a Procore item. Status changes belong to the GC — the owner of the record — and stay that way.

No deletes

The integration performs zero delete operations in Procore. Nothing your team does in BuildCycle can remove anything from the GC’s record.

Formal actions only

Responses post as official RFI replies and submittal approver responses — never as comments somebody on the GC’s side has to re-process by hand.

No duplicate entry

Write the answer once in BuildCycle. Procore receives it programmatically — no retyping, no copy-paste drift between systems.
